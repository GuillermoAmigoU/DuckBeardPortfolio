Rogue One!



Rogue One, A roguelike with Only one stat!


Play it here!

Some friends and I participated on the Game Makers
Toolkit Game Jam 2019, which main theme was "Only One". It was a blast
working with them, and, we came with a very smart idea, which then lead
to some interesting design choices and design.


Our game, Rogue One (pun intended) is a rogue like game,
where all the stats are the same (Roguelike with one value). This means
your health, damage and difficulty, will increase or decrease with the
so-called "value". You lose value by getting hit or buying items at the
store, and gain value by killing enemy and getting their loot. Also, you
have what we called Blessings and Curses, marked on your HUD. These can
give you speed, or fire vulnerability, among others. This creates
different playstyles, from people who stay low on value to have more
speed or people who go "full value" but are very vulnerable to certain
elements.


Obviously we couldn't have all the values of the game
with the same parameter, because dealing 555 damage, having 555 HP and
receiving 555 damage seems a bit boring and plain. Our solution was
making some stats dependant of "the value", and other dependant of the
level. For example, damage, HP, and the blessing/curses were dependant
of this value, but the enemies speed, or attack speed increased with the
level count. This lead us into interesting evolutions, while preserving
the strategy of the game. We also had to make the value influence
differently the game: The HP was linear, '40 value = 40 hp', but what
about the blessing? Having -40% speed was a bit unfunny, so we made some
adjustments, making, such as, logarithmic functions instead of linear
to increase the value, or having a clamped value (for example , you
cannot have more than a 20% movement reduction).


The game was done during 2 days of work with a team of 4
people. I did all sound and music for the game. I participated to the
concept and designed the difficulty curves, made almost all levels and
some HUD elements.


This game as we were told by the reviewers at the jam,
has great potential. This said, we went for the moon, so lots of
features were cut to create a Jam game, like paying to eliminate a
curse. We have plans with this one.
