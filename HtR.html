Hear The Rain


Hear The Rain, an  Audio Game for the visually impaired


Available at the Play Store now!

For my last project at College, I decided to challenge
myself as a Game Designer and as an autodidact Sound Designer. I really
appreciate accessibility in Games, and I wanted to contribute to the list of
accessible games out there. My idea was to make an audio game, using
positional (binaural) sound and tactile input (plus vibration). That's Hear the Rain,
my 'TFG', or final degree project.

Hear The Rain is a puzzle audio game with narrative components where the
player takes the first-person control of Elia, a blind woman who has a
very mysterious neighbor. The mobility is designed as a Dungeon Crawler (snapped in a
3D space), and all puzzles are designed to use different input, motions, and
sound cues, so the game remains varied and uses as much different features of mobile devices as
possible. It is designed as a 30-minutes adventure in which the player
will have to rely on their ears and not their eyes.

The game was done during six months of work with a team
of two people. The team is composed by me (programming, design), and the last 2 months by
Lorena González, a skillful Sound Designer which helped create high-quality
sounds effects for the game. We also had the collaboration of three
Voice Actors to give life to this narrative adventure.
