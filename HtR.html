Hear The Rain


Hear The Rain, a Game for the visually impaired


Available at the Play Store now!

For my last project at College, I decided to challenge
myself, as a Game Designer and as an autodidact Sound Designer. I really
appreciate accessibility in Games, and I wanted to contribute to the
accessible games out there. My idea was to make a game, where the screen
would be black. A game made only with positional (binaural) sound and
tactile input. That's Hear the Rain. All the information about the
project, its creation, investigation and develop will be online shortly
as a 'TFG', or final degree project.


Hear The Rain is a narrative puzzle game where the
player takes the first person control of Elia, a blind person who has a
very peculiar neighbour. This gives Elia lots of curiosity and an
opportunity to snoop around. It is designed as a Dungeon Crawler in
terms of mobility (Movement in a 3D space), but all puzzles are designed
with different input and difficulties, making the game varied and fun
to play. It is designed as a 30-minutes adventure in which the player
will have to rely on their ears and not their eyes.


The game was done during six months of work with a team
of two people. The team is composed by me, and the last 2 months by
Lorena González, a Sound Designer which helped creating high quality
sounds effects for the game. We also had the collaboration of three
Voice Actors to give life to this narrative adventure.
